Selecting labware 
384PP – 384-Well Polypropylene Microplate (PP-0200) 
 
• Low volume aqueous and DMSO transfers (< 2µL per well recommended) 
• Working range: 15-65µL (dependent on fluid type) 
• RNase/DNase free 
 
 
 
384PP Plus – 384-Well Polypropylene Plus Microplate (PPL-0200) 
 
• Low volume aqueous transfers (<2µL per well recommended) 
• Working range; 20-65µL (dependent on fluid type) 
• Available Amine (PPA-0200) or Carboxyl (PPC-0200) coated 
• Distinguishable from the standard Labcyte 384PP plates (PP-0200) by a yellow 
dot (PPA-0200) or green dot (PPC-0200) on the long side of the P24 corner 
• RNase/DNase free 
 
 
384PP TC – 384-Well Polypropylene Tissue Culture Treated Microplate (PPT-0200) 
 
• Low volume aqueous transfers (<2µL per well recommended) 
• Working range; 20-65µL (dependent on fluid type) 
• Adherent cells can be grown, conditioned and lysed in this microplate  
• Amine coated (other treatments available upon request) 
• Sterile with lids 
 
384LDV – 384-Well Cyclic Olefin Copolymer (COC)  Microplate (LP-0200) 
 
• Low volume aqueous and DMSO transfers (<500nL per well recommended) 
• Working range is 2.5-16µl (dependent on fluid type) 
• Constructed of Cyclic Olefin Copolymer (COC) for optimal acoustic transfer 
performance and reagent storage 
• RNase/DNase free

- 11 - 
Preparation of Source Plates 
Correct preparation of the source plate could be essential for reproducible transfers with the Echo®6XX liquid handler. 
Below is a guide to that preparation: 
 
• Allow source plate to thaw/cool to room temperature prior to transfer 
o It may be possible to transfer fluids at alternative temperatures. We would advise performing a drop test 
to determine whether this is applicable to your fluid type 
 
• Centrifugation* of the source plate is not always needed or required. Below are some examples of when 
centrifugation is required;  
o Prevent potential cross contamination of source wells when removing plate seal after storage 
o Bubbles or micro-bubbles** are present in some of the source wells 
 Loading the source plate using reverse pipetting can reduce the formation of bubbles in the source 
plate 
o Excessive wetting of the walls of the source plate 
 Low surface tension fluids (e.g. contains surfactants) can form a steep concave meniscus. This 
could increase the expected dead volume of the source plate 
 
*Recommended centrifugation conditions for aqueous fluids is 1500g for 3 minutes at room temperature 
** Samples containing microbubbles at the bottom of the well might require longer centrifugation times (5 minutes) at 
room temperature without a seal 
Preparation of Intermediate Dilutions in Echo® Source Plates 
Many solvents are readily miscible to form a homogeneous solution without the need for additional mixing in addition to 
centrifugation. Should additional mixing be required e.g. diluting nanoliters of small/large molecules (e.g. oligos, DNA, 
antibodies, peptides) or creating dilutions in a viscous solution the following procedure is recommended:  
 
• Dispense 2.5 or 10µL of the dilution buffer into the 384LDV (LPL-0200) or 384PP (PP-0200 or PPL-0200) plates 
respectively 
• Dispense the small/large molecule 
• Dispense the reminder of the dilution buffer to the required volume 
• Seal and mix the plate for 3 minutes at 1500rpm in a plate shaker (e.g. BioShake 5000 elm) * 
• Centrifuge plate at 1500g for 3 minutes prior to removing the seal and transfer with an Echo®6XX liquid handler 
 
*As the viscosity of the fluids increase the time and rpm may need to be increased proportionality to ensure successful 
homogenization

- 12 - 
Drop Test to Determine the Correct Calibration 
 
Using your selected source plate and calibration transfer fluid into the wells of a flat clear bottom destination plate (e.g. 
384 well Greiner microplate – Cat. No. 781096)    
 
• It is recommended that you transfer from one source well a reasonable volume for visualization (50-200nL) into at 
least 12 destination wells  
 
• Look for presence of uniform droplets at the center of the destination wells (e.g. desired drop) 
o Satellite drops could mean your fluid contains surfactants 
o No drop may indicate that your fluid is outside the capabilities of the calibration 
 
• No drops transferred, analyze the transfer report 
o Please repeat the source plate survey 
o Is the well volume within the working range of the source plate? 
o Is there an error code (see Appendix 2) with the transfer? 
o Was the source plate prepared as outlined in the section; Preparation of Source Plates
